## Risk Management Strategies

Risk cannot be entirely eliminated, only managed. When a risk is identified, a company has four choices:
* **Avoidance:** Stopping the risky activity altogether.
* **Transference:** Shifting the financial impact to a third party (e.g., buying cybersecurity insurance).
* **Mitigation:** Implementing technical or administrative controls to reduce the likelihood or impact of the risk.
* **Acceptance:** Doing nothing because the cost of the safeguard is higher than the potential loss.

## Disaster Recovery Planning (DRP)

When disaster strikes, business continuity depends on two critical metrics:
* **RTO (Recovery Time Objective):** The maximum tolerable amount of time a system can be down before the business suffers catastrophic failure.
* **RPO (Recovery Point Objective):** The maximum amount of data loss (measured in time) the company is willing to accept.

To meet these objectives, companies use alternate processing sites:
* **Hot Site:** Fully operational, synchronized in real-time. Highest cost, near-zero RTO/RPO.
* **Warm Site:** Has infrastructure and hardware, but requires configuration and data restoration.
* **Cold Site:** An empty building with power and internet. Lowest cost, longest recovery time.

## Compliance and Internal Policies

* **Separation of Duties:** A managerial control ensuring no single individual has end-to-end control over a critical process, preventing internal fraud.
* **Regulatory Frameworks:** 
  * **GDPR:** Protects the privacy and PII of EU citizens.
  * **HIPAA:** Protects the PHI (Protected Health Information) of patients in the medical sector.
  * **PCI DSS:** A mandatory standard for any business handling credit card transactions.
